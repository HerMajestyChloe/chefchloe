Blueberry Muffins

Chloe Nelson 07/11/2022

Today I decided to make some of my amazing blueberry muffins. My youngest daughter is obsessed with these muffins, we make them practically every week. I hope that the sweet aroma of baked blueberries and cinnamon filling your kitchen puts you in the best of moods.

These muffins have the perfect texture, and each bite is full of flavor. Store-bought muffins have a dry crumbly texture and are filled with loads of things. These muffins are simple, easy, and have a firm yet soft texture. They will never leave you with more crumbs than a toaster! This recipe will make twelve total muffins that will last anywhere from a few seconds to four days depending on your self-control!

Ingredients:

  1. ½ cup of softened butter $2.04
  2. 1 1/4ths cup of sugar $2.04
  3. 2 eggs $2.04
  4. 1 tsp of vanilla extract $2.04
  5. 2 cups of flour (plus a little extra for blueberries) $2.04
  6. ½ tsp of salt $2.04
  7. 2 tsp of baking powder $2.04
  8. ½ cup of milk $2.04
  9. 2 cups of blueberries that have been washed and sorted through $2.04
  10. 3 tsp of cinnamon sugar $2.04

    11. $2.04 Total for 12 servings!

Directions:

  1. STEP 1: Preheat your oven to 375 degrees.
  2. STEP 2: Mix butter and sugar thoroughly until creamed.
  3. STEP 3: Add eggs one at a time. Beat each egg separately.
  4. STEP 4: Add vanilla to the batter.
  5. STEP 5: Crush ½ cup of blueberries and mix into the batter.
  6. STEP 6: Add remaining blueberries and mix into batter. (To prevent blueberries from sinking to the bottom of the muffins coat the blueberries in some flour!)
  7. STEP 7: In a 12-cup standard muffin tray, apply cupcake liners and fill each to the top with batter.
  8. STEP 8: Sprinkle evenly 3 teaspoons of cinnamon sugar on the tops of each muffin, carefully making sure each muffin gets an even amount.
  9. STEP 9: Bake muffins for 30-35 minutes at 375 degrees.
  10. STEP 10: Remove muffins from the tray and place them on a cooling rack for about 25 minutes….if you can wait that long!
